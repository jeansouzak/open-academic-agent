# Rules for Writing Conclusions and Recommendations (Conclusão)

## Purpose
The conclusion presents the researcher's answers to the research objectives (both general and specific), based entirely on what was identified and analyzed in the Data Presentation chapter. It is the final chapter of the main text.

## Core Principle: Answer the Objectives
The conclusion must directly respond to:
1. Each **specific objective** — summarizing what was found for each one
2. The **general objective** — as a whole, answering the original research question

**The conclusion does NOT introduce new data or theoretical discussions.**

## Required Structure

### Part 1: Restate and Answer the Objectives
- Begin by briefly recalling the research purpose and objectives
- For each specific objective, present the conclusion (answer) derived from Chapter 4
- Close with the answer to the general objective / research question

**Example pattern:**
```
"Through this study, it was possible to [general objective]. Regarding the 
specific objectives:

a) As for [specific objective a], it was found that [conclusion a].
b) Concerning [specific objective b], the study revealed that [conclusion b].
c) In relation to [specific objective c], the research concluded that [conclusion c].

Overall, it was possible to conclude that [answer to the research question]."
```

### Part 2: Researcher's Point of View (Optional but Recommended)
- Express your perspective on the topic and the results found
- This is one of the few places where a more personal tone is acceptable
- Be grounded — opinions must reflect the research findings

### Part 3: Suggestions for Future Research (Recommended)
- Present suggestions for future studies related to the theme
- These can be: deeper explorations of sub-topics, studies in different contexts, use of different methodologies, or investigation of aspects not covered by this research

## Language and Style
- Maintain **impersonal academic tone** (unless briefly offering personal perspective in Part 2)
- Be **concise and objective** — this chapter summarizes, it does not expand
- Avoid repeating entire paragraphs from Chapter 4 — synthesize
- No new citations or theoretical references (everything was already discussed in Chapters 2 and 4)

## What NOT to Include
- Do NOT introduce new data that wasn't analyzed in Chapter 4
- Do NOT include new theoretical citations or references
- Do NOT raise new research questions without suggesting them as future studies
- Do NOT contradict what was found in the analysis chapter
- Do NOT be vague — conclusions must be specific and tied to the objectives

## Example Excerpt (from source document)
> "Through this case study, it was possible to analyze the use of intranet 
> security systems at Organization X. [...] After consolidating, analyzing and 
> interpreting the findings, it was possible to answer all research questions. 
> [...] There is consensus among respondents that the intranet brought advantages 
> such as dissemination, speed, and demystification of information."

## Alignment Reminder
The conclusion must align perfectly with:
- **Chapter 1** (Introduction) — answering the research question and objectives stated there
- **Chapter 4** (Data Analysis) — drawing only from what was found there

If there is misalignment, revisit the previous chapters before finalizing the conclusion.
