# Rules for Writing the Introduction (Introdução)

## Purpose
The introduction contextualizes the research topic, presents the research problem, defines the objectives, justifies the study, and outlines the paper structure.

## Required Structure (in this order)

### 1. Contextualization of the Theme-Problem
- Begin by presenting the general topic and the context in which the research is situated
- Describe the organization or environment being studied (history, characteristics, context)
- Gradually narrow from the general topic toward the specific research problem
- End this section by presenting the **research question** (pergunta de pesquisa)

### 2. Research Objectives
#### General Objective
- Derived directly from the research question
- Written in the affirmative, beginning with a verb in the infinitive
- Common verbs for general objectives: Analyze, Understand, Know, Apply, Synthesize, Evaluate

#### Specific Objectives
- Represent a detailed breakdown of the general objective
- Each specific objective uses an action verb (e.g., identify, describe, investigate, determine, cite)
- Typically 2 to 5 specific objectives
- Together, they should fully answer the general objective

### 3. Justification (Justificativa)
Three required dimensions (based on Roesch, 2009):
- **Importance**: Why is this topic important? For whom? For science?
- **Viability**: Is it feasible to conduct this research? (time, resources, access to sources)
- **Opportunity**: Why is this research timely or relevant right now?

### 4. Paper Organization (optional but recommended)
- A brief paragraph explaining how the paper is structured (what each chapter covers)

## Language and Style Rules
- Use **clear and objective language**
- Maintain **impersonal tone** throughout (avoid first-person)
- Theoretical citations are allowed but should be minimal — the main theory belongs in Chapter 2
- The research question should appear explicitly (as a question)

## Example Structure
```
1 INTRODUCTION

[Contextualization: broad topic → specific context → organization being studied]

[Research question: "How does X present itself in Organization Y?"]

The general objective of this study is to [verb + objective].

The specific objectives are:
a) [verb + specific objective 1]
b) [verb + specific objective 2]
c) [verb + specific objective 3]

[Justification: importance, viability, and opportunity]

[Optional: paper organization paragraph]
```

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Do NOT develop deep theoretical discussions here (save for Chapter 2)
- Do NOT write objectives without an infinitive verb
- Do NOT forget the three dimensions of justification
- Do NOT present data or results in the introduction
- Do NOT use more than 5 specific objectives
