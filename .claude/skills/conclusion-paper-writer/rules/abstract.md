# Rules for Writing the Abstract (Resumo)

## Purpose
The abstract (resumo) is a concise presentation of the paper's key points, allowing the reader to decide whether to consult the full text.

## Required Elements
The abstract must cover these four elements — and ONLY these:
1. **Objective** — what the research aimed to do
2. **Method** — how the research was conducted (approach, instruments used)
3. **Results** — what was found
4. **Conclusions** — what was concluded based on the findings

## Format Rules
- Written as a **single paragraph** (no indented line breaks between ideas)
- **Line spacing**: single (1.0)
- **Word count**: minimum 150, maximum 250 words (for articles/TCCs)
- Font size must match the body text (e.g., Times New Roman 12)
- The title "RESUMO" must be in **bold**
- No citations or references inside the abstract
- Must be **impersonal** — avoid first-person language

## Keywords (Palavras-chave)
- Listed immediately below the abstract
- Approximately 3 keywords
- Separated by periods (.)
- Must be representative of the paper's content
- The expression "Palavras-chave:" must be in **bold**

## Abstract in Foreign Language (Abstract)
- Some institutions or journals require an Abstract in a foreign language (usually English)
- Follows the same formatting rules as the Portuguese abstract
- Keywords in English: "Keywords:"

## Example Structure
```
RESUMO
[Single paragraph covering objective, method, results and conclusions, 
between 150 and 250 words, with no paragraph indentation.]

Palavras-chave: Word1. Word2. Word3.
```

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Do NOT include theoretical discussion or literature review content
- Do NOT use bullet points or numbered lists
- Do NOT break into multiple paragraphs
- Do NOT include tables or figures
- Do NOT go beyond 250 words or under 150 words
